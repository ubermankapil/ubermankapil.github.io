Undergrad



I am currently pursuing my undergraduate education at the Birla Institute of Technology and Science, Pilani, India. It is an institute of national importance and also one of the most prestigious engineering institutes in India. It is known for providing high academic flexibility to its students in terms of electives, minors and especially known for its unique dual degree system. The academic curriculum is pretty challenging and hectic. I attend 30+ hours of lectures and dedicate another 15+ hour for extra academic activities each week. I have been able to maintain consistency in my academic performance at Bits Pilani. I have tried to explored a lot of opportunities in Bits Pilani - Academic Research Work, Clubs and Societies, Sports, Open Source Contribution, Competitive Programming. In essence, I have tried to do a bit of everything. This has helped me discover myself.

Clubs & Societies

I am actively involved with various clubs and societies managed by undergraduate students in my institute. There are regular meetings as a part of these societies where like-minded people come together and discuss various technical problems that they are facing and possible solutions to those problems. There are regular talks where the speaker(s) shares his / her knowledge with the rest of the club members. It provides a perfect platforms for students to learn from their seniors and teach their juniors. Most of the discussion is beyond the academic curriculum of the institute. We also take up projects to learn more about upcoming state of the art technologies. We occasionally hangout together and go for weekend trips to take a break from regular routine. We also have a "trip time".
